About Our Research

The Quantum-Safe Applied Cryptography Research Group conducts research at the intersection of cloud security, blockchain systems, and quantum resilient cryptography. Our goal is to design and rigorously analyze practical cryptographic solutions that protect confidentiality, integrity, and privacy in modern distributed environments.

Our work spans secure cloud computing over outsourced encrypted data, including searchable encryption and protocol design, analysis, and implementation. We investigate blockchain technologies, from consensus level protocols and Layer 2 mechanisms to smart contract and application development, to enhance transparency and accountability in cloud services.

We place strong emphasis on post quantum cryptography, particularly lattice based constructions and their applications, to build systems resilient against quantum adversaries. In parallel, we explore quantum cryptography, including secure communication protocols suitable for quantum computing environments. Across all areas, we perform formal verification and security proofs to ensure robust and provably secure designs.
